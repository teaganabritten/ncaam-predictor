# New Predictive Model Can Predict the Most Unpredictable Tournament

## A New and Complex Model will seek to predict the Men's NCAA Basketball Tournament Results, Something that has never been done in full

## Problem Statement
In close to one hundred years of competition, and many years of attempting to predict it, there has never been an officially-verified submission of a bracket that has predicted either the men's or women's NCAA tournament results in their entirety. March Madness, as it has become known, is expected to come with unexpected results and upsets to teams that seemed to be sure to be successful at the beginning of the tournament. Besides the disappointment of some fans not being able to predict their way to the end, there are other challenges posed by the perceived unpredictability of the tournament. The mens tournament takes place over the course of three to four weeks in March and early April, featuring many different host sites and gaps between games for a team of as short as one day. This makes planning incredibly challenging for event organizers and teams alike. 

## Solution Description
This new model, built and trained using data and calculated metrics from several years of men's college basketball, will use the rich collection of data to predict the outcomes of teams in the tournament. Using the teams performance that season and the historical performance of teams at that seed, the model will output a predicted performance for each team, giving leaders of athletic programs the ability to understand where their teams might finish, what opponents they could face, and what logistics might be involved with the process. Teams could better understand which classes their athletes might miss, what uniforms and equipment they might need and for how long, and which teams to focus on planning for in the tournament. This tool will revolutionize team planning during the busiest period of the season for basketball programs.
