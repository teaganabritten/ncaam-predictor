# New Predictive Model Can Predict the Most Unpredictable Tournament

## A New and Complex Model will seek to predict the Men's NCAA Basketball Tournament Results, Something that has never been done in full

## Problem Statement
In close to one hundred years of competition, and many years of attempting to predict it, there has never been an officially-verified submission of a bracket that has predicted either the men's or women's NCAA tournament results in their entirety. March Madness, as it has become known, is expected to come with unexpected results and upsets to teams that seemed to be sure to be successful at the beginning of the tournament. Besides the dissapointment of some fans not being able to predict their way to the end, there are other challenges posed by the perceived unpredictability of the tournament. The mens tournament takes place over the course of three to four weeks in March and early April, featuring many different host sites and gaps between games for a team of as short as one day. This makes planning incredibly challenging for event organisers and teams alike. 

## Solution Description
This new model, built and trained using data and calculated metrics from several years of men's college basketball, will use the rich collection of data to predict the outcomes of games in the tournament. The output for each game would report which team the model determined to be the winner, an approximate confidence of that team being the winner, and what factor/s would contribute to that team winning. This will provide both an accurate prediction, due to the depth of the analysis and the data driving it, and the explanation of the decisions that will help teams and other groups make decisions based on what the model has decided.
